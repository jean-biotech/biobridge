# Careers in Biotechnology

There's no single path into biotech. Here are the major routes, with realistic entry points.

---

## Research

**What it is:** Discovering new knowledge, developing therapies, studying biological systems

**Where:** Universities, research institutes, pharma companies, government labs

**Entry points:**
- Lab technician (Bachelor's degree)
- Research associate (Bachelor's + experience)
- PhD scientist

**Skills needed:** Experimental design, data analysis, patience, curiosity

---

## Industry / Manufacturing

**What it is:** Scaling up production, quality control, process optimization

**Where:** Biotech companies, pharmaceutical manufacturers, contract manufacturing organizations (CMOs)

**Entry points:**
- Manufacturing associate (High school + training)
- Process engineer (Bachelor's in engineering)
- Quality assurance specialist (Bachelor's in science)

**Skills needed:** Attention to detail, problem-solving, regulatory knowledge

---

## Clinical

**What it is:** Testing therapies in humans, managing trials, patient safety

**Where:** Hospitals, clinical research organizations (CROs), pharma companies

**Entry points:**
- Clinical research coordinator (Bachelor's)
- Clinical trial manager (Bachelor's + experience)
- Medical science liaison (Advanced degree)

**Skills needed:** Organization, communication, ethics, regulatory compliance

---

## Regulatory / Policy

**What it is:** Navigating FDA approval, compliance, public health policy

**Where:** Government agencies (FDA, NIH), consulting firms, pharma companies

**Entry points:**
- Regulatory affairs specialist (Bachelor's)
- Policy analyst (Bachelor's in science/policy)
- Compliance officer (Bachelor's + certifications)

**Skills needed:** Writing, attention to detail, understanding regulations

---

## Business / Operations

**What it is:** Strategy, sales, partnerships, operations, project management

**Where:** Biotech companies, consulting firms, venture capital, startups

**Entry points:**
- Business analyst (Bachelor's in business/science)
- Project manager (Bachelor's + experience)
- Sales representative (Bachelor's)

**Skills needed:** Communication, business acumen, networking

---

## Science Communication

**What it is:** Translating science for public audiences, education, journalism

**Where:** Media outlets, nonprofits, museums, biotech companies

**Entry points:**
- Science writer (Bachelor's + writing portfolio)
- Public engagement coordinator (Bachelor's)
- Medical communications specialist (Bachelor's + experience)

**Skills needed:** Writing, storytelling, scientific literacy
